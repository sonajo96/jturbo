Advancing Innovation from Concept to Commercialization

TRL Definition Key Activities Example for LNG/IBO LNG/Hydrogen Technologies
01 Basic principles observed Theoretical research Understanding the thermodynamic principle behind the twin-expander technology.
02 Technology concept formulated Concept validation Early design of modular liquefaction systems using multi-orthogonal flexibility.
03 Experimental proof of concept Lab-scale testing Testing energy efficiency (e.g., SEC of 5.2 kWh/kg H2) in controlled lab conditions.
04 Technology validated in lab Prototype validation Developing a lab-scale twin-expander prototype with measured performance improvements.
05 Technology validated in relevant environment Pre-commercial prototype testing Testing the modular system in simulated field conditions (e.g., cryogenic environments).
06 Technology demonstrated in relevant environment Pilot-scale testing Deploying the technology in a live pilot project (e.g., 10-15 TPD hydrogen plates or 100-200 TPD LNG/IBO LNG facility).
07 System prototype demonstration in operational environment Field validation Pilots integrated into hydrogen or LNG/IBO LNG operations, validated by customers like L&T, NTFC and are a DOE Hydrogen Hub.
08 System complete and qualified Pre-commercial deployment First small-scale commercial installations (e.g., 1 NTPA LNG/IBO LNG unit in operation).
09 Actual system Proven in operational environment Full-scale commercial operations Large-scale adoption in hydrogen or LNG/IBO LNG facilities with consistent operational and financial performance metrics.
