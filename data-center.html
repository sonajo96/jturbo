Data Center Fueling (LH₂ / LNG Vaporization) and Cryogenic Cooling

Innovative high-efficiency cryogenic thermal management with energy recovery

Leveraging Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG) or Liquid Hydrogen (LH₂) vaporization for cooling AI data centers is an innovative approach that combines high-efficiency cryogenic thermal management with energy recovery.

Both LNG (-162°C) and LH₂ (-253°C) exist as super-cooled liquids, and their phase change from liquid to gas absorbs a large amount of heat. This property can be harnessed for data center cooling before the gases are used as fuel for power generation or other applications.

❄️

Cryogenic Heat Exchange

  • LNG or LH₂ flows through heat exchangers
  • Immersion cooling systems for high-density GPUs/CPUs
  • Direct contact with heat sources for maximum efficiency
🔄

Phase Change Cooling

  • Cryogenic fluid vaporization absorbs excess heat
  • Maintains ultra-low temperatures consistently
  • More efficient than traditional cooling methods
⚡

Energy Recovery Integration

  • Vaporized LNG/LH₂ used as fuel for power generation
  • Closed-loop energy-efficient cooling system
  • Reduces overall energy consumption
🌱

Environmental Benefits

  • Lower carbon footprint compared to conventional cooling
  • Potential for carbon-neutral operations with LH₂
  • Reduced water consumption in cooling processes

Key Benefits

Ultra-Efficient Heat Removal

Cryogenic fluids provide better thermal conductivity than air or traditional liquid cooling.

Reduced Energy Consumption

Eliminates the need for conventional air conditioning, lowering PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness).

Sustainable Cooling + Energy Generation

LH₂ can be used to power fuel cells or gas turbines, making this a dual-purpose system.

Compact and Scalable

Cryogenic cooling reduces space requirements for cooling infrastructure in AI data centers.
